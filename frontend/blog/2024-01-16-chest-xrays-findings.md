# Common Radiological Findings in Chest X-rays

Chest X-rays are among the most commonly ordered radiological examinations. Understanding normal anatomy and common pathological findings is essential for accurate interpretation.

<!-- truncate -->

## Normal Anatomy

- **Lungs**: Well-aerated with visible vascular markings
- **Heart**: Normal size and shape
- **Diaphragm**: Visible and well-defined
- **Bones**: Intact ribs and vertebrae

## Common Abnormalities

- **Pneumonia**: Consolidation in lung tissue
- **Pneumothorax**: Air in pleural space
- **Cardiomegaly**: Enlarged cardiac silhouette
- **Pleural effusion**: Fluid accumulation in pleural space

Accurate interpretation requires systematic evaluation of all visible structures.
