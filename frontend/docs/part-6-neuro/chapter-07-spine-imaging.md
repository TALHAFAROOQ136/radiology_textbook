# Chapter 46: Spine Imaging

## Learning Objectives

- Identify normal spinal anatomy on X-ray, CT, and MRI
- Describe degenerative disc disease imaging features
- Classify disc herniation types
- Recognize spinal stenosis and its causes
- Describe spondylolisthesis and nerve root compression

---

## 46.1 Normal Spinal Anatomy

### Vertebral Anatomy

**Components:**
- Vertebral body: Cancellous bone; anterior element
- Pedicle: Short struts connecting body to posterior elements
- Lamina: Posterior arch
- Spinous process: Posterior projection
- Transverse process: Lateral projections
- Facet (zygapophyseal) joints: Between superior and inferior articular processes

### Disc Anatomy

**Components:**
- **Nucleus pulposus:** Central gelatinous core; high water content; T2 bright on MRI
- **Annulus fibrosus:** Concentric rings of fibrocartilage surrounding nucleus; T2 dark

**Normal disc on MRI (T2):**
- Nucleus: Bright (high water)
- Annulus: Dark (fibrous)
- "Hamburger sign": Bright nucleus between dark endplates

### Spinal Cord and Canal

**Spinal cord ends:** Conus medullaris at L1-L2 level in adults
Below L2: Cauda equina (nerve roots bathed in CSF)

**Normal canal diameter:**
- Cervical: >13 mm
- Lumbar: >15 mm (varies by level)

## 46.2 Degenerative Disc Disease

### Disc Degeneration — MRI Changes

**Modic Endplate Changes:**
| Type | T1 | T2 | Significance |
|------|----|----|-------------|
| **Type I** | Low (dark) | High (bright) | Active inflammation; vascularized fibrous tissue |
| **Type II** | High (bright) | High | Fatty marrow replacement; stable degeneration |
| **Type III** | Low | Low | Dense sclerosis; calcification |

**Disc signal changes:**
- Degenerated disc: T2 signal decreases (desiccated = less water = dark)
- "Black disc": Severely degenerated disc
- Disc height reduction

### Osteophyte Formation

- Bony spurs at vertebral endplate margins
- Anterior osteophytes: Common; usually asymptomatic
- Posterior osteophytes: May narrow canal or foramina → nerve compression

### Facet Joint Degeneration

- Joint space narrowing
- Articular surface irregularity
- Facet hypertrophy
- Effusion (facet arthritis)
- Ligamentum flavum hypertrophy → posterolateral canal narrowing

## 46.3 Disc Herniation

### Terminology (North American Spine Society)

**Disc bulge:**
- Diffuse (>180°) extension beyond disc space
- Symmetric; not a herniation

**Herniation subtypes (focal, &lt;25% of disc circumference):**

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Protrusion** | Herniated material has base wider than displaced portion |
| **Extrusion** | Herniated material extends beyond base; "waist" formed |
| **Sequestration** | Extruded fragment loses connection to parent disc (free fragment) |

**Migration:** Fragment moves away from disc level (up or down)

### Direction of Herniation

| Location | Effect |
|----------|--------|
| **Central** | Spinal cord (cervical) or cauda equina (lumbar) compression |
| **Paracentral (posterolateral)** | Traversing nerve root compression (at or below disc level) |
| **Foraminal** | Exiting nerve root at that level |
| **Far lateral (extraforaminal)** | Exiting nerve root lateral to foramen |

### MRI Features

**T2 MRI:**
- Herniated disc material: Similar to disc (dark on T2 if degenerated, intermediate signal)
- CSF displacement (loss of bright CSF signal around cord/roots)
- Nerve root impingement: Root displaced or compressed by herniated material

**Sagittal view:** Best for disc level and craniocaudal extent
**Axial view:** Best for canal and foraminal encroachment

### Clinical Correlation

**Lumbar disc herniation:**
- L4-L5 (most common): Affects L5 nerve root (paracentral)
- L5-S1: Affects S1 nerve root
- Paracentral herniation compresses the nerve root exiting ONE LEVEL BELOW the disc

**Cervical disc herniation:**
- C5-C6 most common: C6 nerve root
- C6-C7: C7 nerve root
- Central herniation: Myelopathy (cord compression)

## 46.4 Spinal Stenosis

### Types

**Central canal stenosis:**
- AP diameter of spinal canal reduced
- Lumbar: &lt;10 mm = severe
- Cervical: &lt;10 mm = severe

**Foraminal stenosis:**
- Neural foramen narrowed
- Causes: Facet hypertrophy, disc herniation, osteophytes

**Causes:**
- Degenerative (most common): Disc bulge + facet hypertrophy + ligamentum flavum hypertrophy
- Congenital: Short pedicles (narrow canal from birth)
- Ossification of posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL) — cervical; more common in Asian populations

### MRI Features

**Lumbar central stenosis:**
- Reduction of CSF space around cauda equina
- Nerve roots clumped together
- AP diameter measurement

**Cervical stenosis:**
- Cord compression: Cord flattened or compressed
- T2 cord signal change (myelopathy): Indicates cord injury
- T2 bright signal in cord (intramedullary) = chronic myelopathy

**Neurogenic claudication (lumbar stenosis):**
- Bilateral leg pain, weakness with walking; relieved by sitting/bending forward
- Distinguished from vascular claudication by: position-dependence; normal peripheral pulses

## 46.5 Spondylolisthesis

### Definition

Anterior (or occasionally posterior) displacement of one vertebral body relative to the adjacent inferior vertebra.

### Classification (Meyerding)

- Grade I: &lt;25% slip
- Grade II: 25-50%
- Grade III: 50-75%
- Grade IV: 75-100%
- Grade V (spondyloptosis): >100%

### Types by Cause

**Degenerative (most common in adults):**
- Facet joint degeneration → instability
- Most common at L4-L5
- Intact pars interarticularis

**Isthmic (spondylolytic):**
- Pars interarticularis defect (spondylolysis)
- Most common at L5-S1
- Athletes (gymnasts, cricketers)

**Traumatic:**
- Posterior element fracture

**Dysplastic (congenital):**
- Deficient superior sacral facets

### MRI/CT of Spondylolisthesis

- Sagittal view: Shows vertebral body displacement relative to next
- CT: Pars defect (spondylolysis) — oblique reconstruction essential
- MRI: Neural foraminal stenosis secondary to listhesis; disc degeneration; cord/root compression

---

## Key Points Summary

1. **Modic Type I endplate change** — active inflammation (T1 dark, T2 bright); Type II = fatty change (T1 bright)

2. **Disc herniation types:** Protrusion (broad base), Extrusion (narrow waist/base), Sequestration (free fragment)

3. **Paracentral (posterolateral) herniation** at L4-L5 compresses the L5 root (traversing root below that disc)

4. **Ligamentum flavum + facet hypertrophy + disc bulge** = three-component degenerative canal stenosis

5. **Cervical myelopathy** — T2 bright signal in cord = chronic compression damage; indicates severity

6. **Spondylolisthesis** — isthmic type from pars defect (L5-S1); degenerative type from facet arthritis (L4-L5)
