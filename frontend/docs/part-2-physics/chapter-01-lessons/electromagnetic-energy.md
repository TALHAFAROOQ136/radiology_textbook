# Electromagnetic Energy

Electromagnetic (EM) energy is the foundation of most medical imaging modalities. Understanding its nature, properties, and behavior is essential for radiologic science.

## Nature of Electromagnetic Radiation

Electromagnetic radiation has a **dual nature**:

### Wave Model
- Travels as oscillating electric and magnetic fields perpendicular to each other and to the direction of propagation
- Characterized by **wavelength (λ)** and **frequency (f)**
- All EM waves travel at the speed of light in a vacuum: **c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s**

**Wave equation**: c = f × λ

### Particle (Quantum) Model
- Energy is carried in discrete packets called **photons**
- Energy of a photon: **E = hf = hc/λ**
  - h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s (Planck's constant)
- Higher frequency → higher energy → more penetrating

## The Electromagnetic Spectrum

| Region | Wavelength | Frequency | Energy | Medical Use |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Radio waves | &gt;1 m | &lt;300 MHz | &lt;1 µeV | MRI |
| Microwaves | 1 m – 1 mm | 300 MHz–300 GHz | µeV range | Diathermy |
| Infrared | 1 mm – 700 nm | 300 GHz–430 THz | meV range | Thermography |
| Visible light | 700–400 nm | 430–750 THz | ~2 eV | Endoscopy |
| Ultraviolet | 400–10 nm | 750 THz–30 PHz | 3–120 eV | Phototherapy |
| X-rays | 10 nm – 1 pm | 30 PHz–30 EHz | 0.1 keV–120 keV | Radiography, CT |
| Gamma rays | &lt;1 pm | &gt;30 EHz | &gt;100 keV | Nuclear medicine |

## Properties of X-rays

X-rays are the primary EM radiation used in diagnostic imaging:

- **Invisible** to the naked eye
- **Travel in straight lines** at the speed of light
- **Cannot be focused** by lenses (unlike visible light)
- **Penetrate matter** — degree depends on energy and material
- **Ionize matter** — can remove electrons from atoms
- **Affect photographic film** and digital detectors
- **Cause fluorescence** in certain materials
- **Biologically active** — can damage DNA

## Inverse Square Law

The intensity of radiation decreases with the square of the distance from the source:

**I₁/I₂ = (d₂/d₁)²**

Or equivalently: **I ∝ 1/d²**

**Clinical application**: Doubling the distance from an X-ray source reduces intensity to **one quarter**.

| Distance from source | Relative intensity |
|---|---|
| 1 m | 100% |
| 2 m | 25% |
| 3 m | 11% |

This principle is fundamental to radiation protection for radiographers.

## Coherence and Polarization

- X-rays produced in the X-ray tube are **incoherent** (random phase)
- X-rays are **unpolarized** (oscillate in all planes)
- Contrast with laser light: coherent and polarized

## Energy Conversion in X-ray Production

In the X-ray tube, electrical energy is converted:

**Electrical energy → Kinetic energy (electrons) → X-ray energy + Heat**

- Only ~1% of electron energy converts to X-rays
- ~99% is lost as heat — reason for anode cooling requirements
