# Radiation

X-rays are produced when high-energy electrons interact with matter, converting kinetic energy into electromagnetic radiation.

## The X-ray Tube

The X-ray tube consists of two main electrodes in an evacuated glass or metal envelope:

- **Cathode**: Negatively charged electrode containing a tungsten filament that emits electrons via thermionic emission
- **Anode**: Positively charged electrode (target) that receives electrons and produces X-rays

## Mechanisms of X-ray Production

### 1. Bremsstrahlung (Braking Radiation)
- **Mechanism**: Electrons decelerate as they pass near the nucleus of a target atom
- **Energy**: Continuous spectrum from zero up to the peak kilovoltage (kVp)
- **Proportion**: ~80–90% of X-ray output

### 2. Characteristic Radiation
- **Mechanism**: Incident electron ejects an inner-shell electron; outer-shell electron fills the vacancy, emitting a photon of specific energy
- **Energy**: Discrete energies characteristic of the target material
- **Proportion**: ~10–20% of X-ray output (only above threshold energy)

## Factors Affecting X-ray Output

| Factor | Effect on Output | Effect on Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **kVp** (kilovoltage peak) | ↑ kVp → ↑↑ output | Higher energy, more penetrating |
| **mA** (milliamperage) | ↑ mA → ↑ output | No change in quality |
| **mAs** (mA × time) | Proportional to output | No change in quality |
| **Target material** (Z) | Higher Z → more efficient | Higher characteristic energies |
| **Filtration** | ↓ output | Removes low-energy photons |

## Anode Characteristics

- **Rotating anode**: Distributes heat over a larger area; allows higher mA settings
- **Focal spot size**: Smaller focal spot → better spatial resolution; larger focal spot → higher heat capacity
- **Anode angle**: Affects field coverage and effective focal spot size (line focus principle)

## X-ray Beam Filtration

Filtration removes low-energy (non-diagnostic) photons that increase patient dose without contributing to the image:

- **Inherent filtration**: From the glass envelope and oil (~0.5–1.0 mm Al equivalent)
- **Added filtration**: Aluminum or copper sheets placed in the beam path
- **Total filtration**: Required minimum of 2.5 mm Al equivalent for general radiography

## Summary

| Parameter | Controls |
|---|---|
| kVp | Beam energy (quality), contrast |
| mAs | Number of photons (quantity), density |
| Filtration | Beam hardening, dose reduction |
| Focal spot | Spatial resolution |
