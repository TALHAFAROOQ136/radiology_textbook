# Screen-Film Radiographic Quality Control

Quality Control (QC) for screen-film systems involves a structured programme of tests on film, screens, cassettes, processors, and viewing equipment to ensure consistent diagnostic image quality.

## QC Programme Components

A comprehensive screen-film QC programme includes:
1. Processor quality control (sensitometry)
2. Screen and cassette maintenance
3. Darkroom integrity testing
4. Viewbox and viewing conditions
5. Repeat/reject analysis
6. X-ray equipment testing

---

## Processor Quality Control

The most critical and frequently performed QC test in screen-film radiography.

### Sensitometry
Evaluates the processor's performance by exposing a film strip to a known range of light exposures using a **sensitometer**, then measuring density with a **densitometer**.

**Sensitometer**: Device that exposes film to a standardised series of light steps.
**Densitometer**: Optical instrument measuring optical density (OD) of film.

### Control Chart Parameters

| Parameter | Definition | Acceptable Variation |
|---|---|---|
| Base + Fog (B+F) | Minimum density of unexposed film | ±0.03 OD from established level |
| Speed index | Density at mid-exposure step (~1.0 OD above B+F) | ±0.10 OD |
| Contrast index | Difference between high and low density steps | ±0.10 OD |

**Daily action limits**: If any parameter exceeds limits, do not use processor for clinical work until problem is identified and corrected.

### Replenishment Rates
- Developer and fixer solutions must be replenished to maintain chemical activity
- Insufficient replenishment → underdevelopment, decreased speed and contrast
- Excessive replenishment → overdevelopment, increased fog

## Screen and Cassette QC

### Cleaning
- **Frequency**: Monthly (or when artifacts are detected)
- **Method**: Approved screen-cleaning solution and lint-free cloth; allow to dry completely before closing
- **Importance**: Dust and debris → white artifacts on images

### Screen-Film Contact Test
- **Method**: Expose wire mesh phantom placed on cassette; process and evaluate
- **Acceptance**: No areas of unsharpness
- **Frequency**: Semi-annually or when contact artifacts are suspected
- **Action**: Replace cassette if poor contact cannot be corrected

### Cassette Inspection
- Check for light leaks (darken room; inspect for light ingress)
- Check hinge and latch integrity
- Check screen surface for scratches, stains, or phosphor loss

### Screen Speed Matching
- All cassettes of the same type should have matched screen speeds
- Unmatched speeds → different densities for same exposure → inconsistency
- Test: Expose all cassettes with same technique; compare densities

## Darkroom Quality Control

### Safelight Testing
Verifies safelight does not fog film:
- **Method**: Place exposed film on counter; cover half; expose to safelight for 2 minutes; process
- **Acceptance**: No visible density difference between covered and uncovered halves
- **Check**: Correct filter colour for film type; bulb wattage not exceeded; adequate distance from work surface

### Darkroom Fog Test
- Expose film to 0.5 OD; place in darkroom for 2 minutes; process
- Acceptable fog increase: &lt;0.05 OD above original density

### Light Leak Test
- Stay in darkroom for 5 minutes until eyes adapt to darkness
- Inspect all edges, door seals, pass boxes, and ventilation openings for light leaks

## Viewbox Quality Control

### Luminance Testing
- **Standard**: All viewboxes in a department should have equal, uniform luminance
- **Measurement**: Photometer/luminance meter
- **Typical luminance**: 1,500–3,500 cd/m²
- **Uniformity**: &lt;10–15% variation across surface

### Viewing Conditions
- Clean viewbox surfaces (marks reduce contrast)
- Mask unexposed areas with black card (reduces glare, improves perceived contrast)
- Ambient light should be low (no bright windows behind radiologist)

---

## Repeat/Reject Analysis

### Process
1. Collect all repeated/rejected films over a defined period (typically 1 month)
2. Categorise by reason (positioning, exposure, artifacts, equipment)
3. Calculate repeat rate: (repeats ÷ total films) × 100%
4. Target repeat rate: &lt;5–10%

### Benefits
- Identifies training needs
- Highlights equipment problems
- Reduces unnecessary patient dose
- Improves department efficiency

## QC Testing Frequency Summary

| Test | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Processor sensitometry | Daily |
| Darkroom cleanliness | Daily |
| Safelight test | Semi-annually |
| Screen cleaning | Monthly |
| Screen-film contact | Semi-annually |
| Cassette inspection | Semi-annually |
| Viewbox luminance | Annually |
| Repeat/reject analysis | Monthly |
