# Screen-Film Radiographic Artifacts

Screen-film artifacts are unwanted densities or patterns on a radiographic image caused by problems with the film, intensifying screens, cassette, or processing. Recognising these artifacts is essential for quality radiographic practice.

## Film-Related Artifacts

### Fogging
Unwanted overall increase in film density:

| Cause | Appearance |
|---|---|
| Light leak (cassette or darkroom) | Uniform or patchy increased density |
| Radiation fog (scatter, background) | Uniform grey veil over image |
| Heat or humidity | Overall greyness, reduced contrast |
| Outdated film | Increased base fog density |
| Safelight fog | Faint overall density increase |

**Prevention**: Inspect cassettes for light leaks, store film properly, check safelight filter integrity.

### Static Artifacts
Caused by electrostatic discharge during film handling:

| Type | Appearance |
|---|---|
| Crown static | Radiating branching dark lines |
| Tree static | Tree-branch pattern of dark lines |
| Smudge static | Irregular dark smudge |

**Prevention**: Humidity control in darkroom (40–60% RH), handle film carefully, anti-static screens.

### Pressure Artifacts
- **Crescent (fingernail) marks**: Dark crescentic marks from bending film sharply
- **Pressure marks**: Dark or light spots from excessive pressure on film before processing
- **Cassette pressure**: Uneven screen-film contact causing blurring

### Handling Artifacts
| Artifact | Cause |
|---|---|
| Fingerprints | Skin oils on film surface before processing |
| Scratches | Sharp objects contacting film emulsion |
| Hesitation marks | Film stopping briefly in processor rollers |
| Cinch marks | Film pulled tight, causing parallel lines |

## Screen-Related Artifacts

### Screen Damage
| Artifact | Cause | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| Scratches | Physical damage to phosphor coating | White (unexposed) lines |
| Dirt / debris | Foreign material on screen surface | White spots or artefacts |
| Screen fog | Phosphorescent afterglow | Increased density in previous exposure area |

### Poor Screen-Film Contact
- **Cause**: Warped cassette, worn padding, trapped air
- **Appearance**: Blurred area(s) on image
- **Test**: Wire mesh contact test — blurred areas indicate poor contact
- **Prevention**: Regular cassette inspection, replace worn cassettes

## Processing Artifacts

### Chemical Artifacts
| Artifact | Cause | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| Developer cutoff | Insufficient developer level | Sharp line of density change |
| Fixer retention | Inadequate washing | Yellow-brown staining over time |
| Chemical fog | Contaminated developer | Overall increased density |
| Dichroic fog | Developer contamination with fixer | Pink/green colour in transmitted light |

### Mechanical (Processor) Artifacts
| Artifact | Cause | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| Roller marks | Dirty or damaged transport rollers | Periodic bands perpendicular to film travel |
| Hesitation marks | Film stops in processor | Dark bands parallel to leading edge |
| Pi lines | Dirty roller at fixed position | Lines at intervals = roller circumference (~3.14 × diameter) |
| Chatter marks | Loose or worn rollers | Fine regular lines across film |

### Temperature Artifacts
- **Overdevelopment** (too hot): Dark, low contrast image
- **Underdevelopment** (too cold): Light, low contrast image
- **Uneven temperature**: Patchy density variation

---

## Exposure Artifacts

| Artifact | Cause | Appearance |
|---|---|---|
| Double exposure | Two exposures on same film | Superimposed images |
| Underexposure | Insufficient mAs or kVp | Light, noisy image |
| Overexposure | Excessive mAs or kVp | Dark image, loss of contrast |
| Grid lines | Stationary grid or Bucky failure | Fine parallel lines across image |
| Grid cut-off | Misaligned focused grid | Loss of density at edges or centre |

## Summary Table

| Category | Common Artifacts | Prevention |
|---|---|---|
| Film handling | Static, pressure, fingerprint marks | Careful handling, humidity control |
| Screen | Scratches, dirt, poor contact | Regular cleaning, contact testing |
| Processing | Roller marks, pi lines, chemical fog | Processor QC, solution replenishment |
| Exposure | Grid artifacts, double exposure, fog | Correct technique, cassette inspection |
