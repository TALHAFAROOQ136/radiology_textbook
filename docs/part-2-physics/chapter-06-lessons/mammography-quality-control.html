Mammography Quality Control

Mammography QC is among the most rigorous in diagnostic radiology due to the high spatial and contrast resolution demands, the sensitive patient population, and regulatory requirements. Comprehensive QC programmes are mandatory for accreditation.

Regulatory Framework​

OrganisationStandard
ACR (USA)Mammography Quality Standards Act (MQSA) — federally mandated
EUREF (Europe)European guidelines for quality assurance in breast cancer screening
IPEM / RCR (UK)National guidelines for mammography QC

MQSA (USA) requires:

  • Accreditation by an approved body (ACR)
  • Annual medical physicist survey
  • Daily, weekly, and monthly QC tests by radiographer
  • Documented QC records retained for specific periods

Radiographer QC Tests​

Daily Tests​

Phantom Image Evaluation:

  • Expose ACR accreditation phantom (or equivalent)
  • Phantom contains simulated masses, fibres, and calcification clusters
  • Minimum score required (e.g., ACR: 4 fibres, 3 masses, 3 calcification clusters visible)
  • Evaluate: contrast, noise, artifacts, spatial resolution

Visual Checklist:

  • Compression paddle condition
  • Cassette/detector surface cleanliness
  • Compression force display functioning
  • Collimation accuracy
  • Monitor display check

Weekly Tests (Screen-Film)​

  • Darkroom cleanliness: Free from dust and debris
  • Processor QC: Sensitometry — speed, contrast, base+fog within limits

Monthly Tests​

  • Visual checklist (comprehensive version)
  • Compression force: Verify compression device reaches 111–200 N
  • Darkroom fog: Exposure <0.05 OD increase after 2-minute safelight exposure (screen-film)

Medical Physicist Annual Survey​

Comprehensive testing performed by a qualified medical physicist:

Image Quality Tests​

TestPurpose
Phantom image qualityMinimum detectable object sizes
Spatial resolutionMTF measurement; minimum 11–13 lp/mm
Low-contrast detectabilityContrast-detail phantom
Noise assessmentUniform field image analysis

Dose Tests​

TestPurpose
Mean glandular dose (MGD)Must not exceed 3 mGy per view (standard breast)
Entrance surface air kermaMeasured with ionisation chamber
AEC performanceConsistent dose across range of breast thicknesses and densities

Equipment Tests​

TestAcceptance Criterion
kVp accuracyWithin ±5% of indicated value
HVL measurementAppropriate for kVp and target/filter combination
Focal spot sizeWithin tolerance (0.3 mm nominal)
Compression force111–200 N
Beam alignmentField within 2% of SID
AEC reproducibilityCV <5%

Display Monitor Tests (Digital Mammography)​

TestCriterion
Maximum luminance>600 cd/m²
Luminance ratio>250:1
DICOM GSDF calibrationWithin tolerance
Uniformity<15% variation
Spatial resolutionMinimum 5 lp/mm at display

Digital Mammography-Specific QC​

Detector Uniformity (Flat-Field)​

  • Acquire uniform exposure image
  • Analyse for dead pixels, sensitivity non-uniformity, artifacts
  • Frequency: Daily/weekly

Exposure Index Monitoring​

  • Monitor EI for every patient examination
  • Detect dose creep or underexposure
  • Target: Within defined range for each breast thickness

Detector Calibration​

  • Gain and offset correction maps updated regularly
  • Full recalibration after detector replacement or major service
  • Frequency: As directed by manufacturer / after service

Ghost Image Evaluation​

  • Expose detector to high dose; immediately acquire low-dose uniform image
  • Check for residual image from previous exposure
  • Frequency: Weekly

Reject/Repeat Analysis​

Frequency: Monthly

Mammography-specific categories:

  • Positioning errors (most common)
  • Motion unsharpness
  • Artifacts (compression paddle, detector)
  • Inadequate compression
  • Exposure errors

Acceptable repeat rate: <3% in screening programmes

QC Record Keeping​

All QC results must be:

  • Recorded contemporaneously
  • Signed by the performing radiographer
  • Reviewed by lead radiographer and medical physicist
  • Retained for minimum 5 years (MQSA: life of equipment + 1 year)
  • Available for regulatory inspection at any time

QC Failure — Action Protocol​

SituationAction
Phantom score below minimumDo not perform clinical mammography; investigate and correct
Processor out of control (screen-film)Do not use processor; correct and retest
Monitor luminance below thresholdDo not use for primary reporting; service/replace
MGD above limitSuspend clinical use; investigate; notify regulator
EI persistently outside rangeReview technique chart; retrain staff
